\section{Transferability to extended tasks}
In Section~\ref{sec:umms}, we observed that knowledge integration between understanding and generation occurs in UMMs with certain architectural properties, and that this can be verified through cross-task transferability. While the quantity-aware reasoning experiments confirm that transfer occurs bi-directionally, whether other tasks exhibit the same pattern, or whether transfer favors a particular direction, remains unclear. In this section, we investigate this by extending our analysis to two additional tasks: (1) OCR, where understanding and generation share character-level visual patterns, and (2) spatial position, where both tasks rely on reasoning about relative object locations.
